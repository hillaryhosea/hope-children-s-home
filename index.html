About US

Welcome to Hope Children’s Home—a sanctuary where every child is nurtured to reach their fullest potential. Here, we believe that childhood should be filled with laughter, learning, and the security of knowing you are loved. Founded on the conviction that every young life deserves compassion, stability, and opportunity, our home stands as a beacon of hope for those who have endured hardship, abandonment, or loss.

From the moment a child arrives, they become part of our family, embraced by dedicated caregivers, educators, and counselors who work together to support their emotional well-being and developmental milestones. We provide more than just a safe roof and daily meals—we offer a foundation for a brighter future. Through structured routines, engaging educational activities, therapeutic support, and life-skills workshops, each child is empowered to grow with confidence and resilience.

Our Three Pillars of Hope

At Hope Children’s Home, we’re guided by three foundational missions. We Rescue vulnerable children into a safe, caring environment;
we Educate them through tailored learning and life-skills programs; and we Heal their hearts and minds with compassionate support and health services.

Success Stories

Every day at Hope Children’s Home, children move from uncertainty to confidence, finding safety, learning, and joy.
Their stories show that with care and opportunity, every child can truly thrive.

Grace Njeri – Age 17

Before I came to the children’s home, I had no idea what my future would look like. But here, I found a family and a purpose. With their support, I returned to school and recently scored top marks in my national exams. I now dream of becoming a lawyer to fight for children’s rights—just like someone once fought for mine.

Samuel Otieno – Age 16

When I lost my parents, I thought my life was over. The children’s home took me in, gave me love, and helped me discover my talent in football. Today, I play for my school team and was recently selected for a regional youth league. I want to make it to the national team and give back to the place that believed in me.

Aisha Mwende – Age 15

I used to struggle with confidence, but the home gave me a second chance. Through mentorship and encouragement, I’ve blossomed into a strong student and an active peer leader. I now mentor younger girls and hope to become a teacher one day. This place gave me more than shelter—it gave me purpose.

Brian Kipkoech – Age 18

Coming here changed my life. I was introduced to computers and digital skills that I never had access to before. With the help of volunteers, I learned basic coding and graphic design. I’m now preparing to join a technical college to study ICT. I want to start a tech club for street kids one day.

Cynthia Wambui – Age 14

At the children’s home, I found more than a safe place—I found healing. I love music, and through the support of the staff, I’ve been writing songs and performing in events. Music helps me express myself, and one day, I hope to inspire others through my voice. This home helped me find my spark.

Kevin Muriithi – Age 17

I was always told I’d never make it. But here, they saw something in me I didn’t. I’ve since become a top student in math and science, and I recently won a science fair project on clean energy. I plan to become an engineer and build solutions that help underserved communities. I owe it all to the love and support I found here.
